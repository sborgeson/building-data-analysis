User testing

This tool was developed at Lawrence Berkieley National Lab with funding from the US Department of Energy to provide an open, documented, and easy to use tool for the analysis of of Smart Meter data. It is intended to support ongoing improvements in the methods and tools used to make sense of Smart Meter data, to improve building owner and occupant access to sound analytical methods, and to support better decision making about energy efficiency interventions. It is intended as a gateway to rather than a substitute for more detailed professional assessments.

You have been selected to participate in testing the tool. Would you please take 10-30 minutes to use it and provide valuable feedback on your experience with it? In that time, you will be asked to locate and upload your smart meter data along with some basic building information. A custom pdf report based on your data will be generated and made available for you to download and you will be asked to answer some basic questions about your experience. In return, you will quite possibly find ways to better understand your energy use and save money on your future utility bills!

  • First you will need to locate and download your own Smart Meter data. We have provided general instructions for getting your Green Button data. However, if you have gotten stuck along the way, you can download a sample data file to use as your own.
